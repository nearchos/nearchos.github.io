Ph.D. Topic

Quick prototyping of assistive technology applications for mobile devices

Why a PhD at UCLan UK?

  • Tuition is waived for the three-years (thanks to a sponsorship by UCLan UK)
  • Be part of a 700-strong community of student researchers and join them yearly in the annual research conference at Preston, UK
  • Receive your degree from UCLan UK, a World-class research organization.

How and when to apply?

The application deadline is 28th June 2013. More information, and the required application forms are available here. The accepted student will start their studies October 1st, 2013.

What is the topic about?

Assistive Technology is utilizing the increasing sensing capabilities of modern smartphones to enable novel AT applications (i.e. apps that assist people with motor disabilities). This project will develop a smartphone-based platform and methodology for quick prototyping of such applications by employing reusable software components.

What else do I need to know?

Inventor Charles Franklin Kettering (1876-1958) argued that

''A problem well stated is a problem half solved''

He was probably right. Along these lines, this page does not provide a very thorough description of this PhD topic, but rather a rough description of it. The main goal of this page is to help interested applicants decide if this is something they would like to spend the next 3 years (minimum) of their life working on.

The title of the PhD topic is Quick prototyping of assistive technology applications for mobile devices As a PhD candidate pursuing this topic you would be expected to:

  • Review state of the art in AT (Assistive Technology) and identify the limitations related to creating quick prototypes of AT apps using commercial components (both software and hardware)
  • Develop a pluggable mechanism for quick prototyping of AT applications, centered on a mobile device
  • Develop a description and modeling language that allows the formation of AT apps using an appropriate editor and a plugin repository
  • Utilize cloud technology to model and implement the AT apps

Planning

Generally, the first six months are spent at clarifying and finalizing the Ph.D. project title and content, and putting together a detailed plan for the remaining two and a half years. Students are expected to meet with their supervisors weekly or biweekly, to report progress, seek help on blocking issues, and refine the plan as needed.

Supervision

How do you expect your supervision to be like? The main supervisor would be myself, but you would also be co-supervised by faculty based at UCLan UK. You would also be expected to travel to Preston once a year for a Ph.D. workshop.

Practically, Matt Welsh's comments best describe how I would like to supervise:

''If I were *forced* to go back to academia, I would run my group much more like a software team. We would have a clear set of objectives and milestones; we'd do code reviews; we'd all sit in the same lab together (I'd eschew a separate office except for one on one meetings); we'd do regular performance reviews for everyone (at least quarterly). The expectations would be made very clear about what it would take to be successful. I would no longer allow students to meander in twice a week and get nothing done for months on end until the next paper deadline rolls around. And we'd focus on "shipping", both code and papers. I would quickly kill projects that were not gaining traction. I was pretty bad about that and let some projects drag on for years without any tangible results (mostly because I had a grant and there was no downside, apart from the mental load.)''
